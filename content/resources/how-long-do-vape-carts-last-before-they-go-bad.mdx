## How Long Do Vape Carts Last?

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
				

### Unopened (Stored Well) One question we get is whether vape carts expire if unopened. If your vape cart is still sealed and stored in a cool, dark place, it can stay fresh for quite a while.

Distillate carts usually last 12–24 months without much loss of potency or flavor. They’re stripped down to mostly [cannabinoids](https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/cannabis-marijuana-and-cannabinoids-what-you-need-to-know)[,](https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/cannabis-marijuana-and-cannabinoids-what-you-need-to-know) which makes them stable—like jars of honey that barely seem to age.

Live resin, rosin, and terpene-heavy carts, on the other hand, are more delicate. Those flavorful [terpenes](https://kinebudsdispensary.com/blog/uncategorized/cannabis-terpenes-everything-you-need-to-know/) that make your cart taste and smell amazing? They also degrade faster. Think of them as fresh juice: delicious at peak freshness but best enjoyed within **6–12 months**

		
				

### Opened (Now Getting Love) Once you break the seal, your cart is exposed to oxygen, light, and heat, which all speed up the breakdown. Most opened carts are at their best for **6–12 months**. Past that, they still work, but the flavor may flatten, the oil can darken, and the high may feel weaker.

It’s not usually unsafe—just underwhelming. Think of it like soda: still drinkable when flat, but nowhere near as satisfying. [Project CBD (cannabinoids)](https://projectcbd.org/).

		
									

### Quick FAQ: Is a year-old cart safe to hit?

									

If it was stored properly and still looks and smells normal, it’s usually safe—but don’t expect peak effects. If it smells burnt, tastes harsh, or looks contaminated, skip it. [ScienceDaily (cannabis news)](https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/mind_brain/cannabis/).

## Why There’s No One-Size-Fits-All “Expiration Date”

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
                Here’s the kicker: vape carts don’t come with universal expiration dates. Their shelf life depends on a mix of factors:             [PubMed Central (free full text)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/).
				

### Oil type Distillate is the most stable; live resin and rosin fade faster.

				

### Terpene levels More terps = better flavor, but they evaporate quickly.

				

### Hardware quality Well-sealed carts last longer than cheap ones.

				

### Storage conditions Heat, light, and oxygen all speed up degradation.

				

### Climate Constant temperature swings or humidity can shorten shelf life.

## FAQs

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
							01. Do vape carts expire?						 [Project CBD (cannabinoids)](https://projectcbd.org/).
						

Yes—they don’t spoil like milk, but potency and flavor fade. Unopened carts can remain serviceable for up to **two years** in ideal storage conditions; after that, they may be safe to hit but lack punch. Once opened? Expect quality to decrease within **6–12 months**, especially if not stored properly. [FDA (vaping updates)](https://www.fda.gov/tobacco-products).

					
							02. Is it safe to hit a brown cart?						 [CDC (cannabis and health)](https://www.cdc.gov/cannabis/index.html).
						

Brown discoloration is usually oxidation, not necessarily dangerous. If it smells and tastes clean, it may still be okay. But if it tastes bitter, harsh, or oily, that’s your cue to retire it. [ScienceDaily (cannabis news)](https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/mind_brain/cannabis/).

					
							03. Should I refrigerate carts?						 [NORML (policy overview)](https://norml.org/).
						

Nope. Refrigeration can cause condensation, clog your cart, and invite moisture back in when you remove it. Stick to **room temperature in a dark, cool spot**, and keep it upright. [CDC (vaping & lung injury)](https://www.cdc.gov/tobacco/basic_information/e-cigarettes/severe-lung-disease.html).

					
							04. Why did my CBD cart crystallize?						 [National Institute on Drug Abuse (cannabis)](https://nida.nih.gov/research-topics/marijuana).
						

Crystals often mean purity—not degradation. High concentrations of CBD can form harmless crystals, especially when cold. Gently warming the cart (like with your hand) should re-liquefy it. [PubMed Central (free full text)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/).

					
							05. Can carts go bad in a hot car?						 [Cleveland Clinic (marijuana)](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/drugs/21562-marijuana-cannabis).
						

Absolutely. Heat speeds up oxidation, degrades terpenes and cannabinoids, and can cause leaks. Your cup holder is not a storage solution—keep carts cool and shaded instead. [Leafly (science section)](https://www.leafly.com/news/science-tech).
