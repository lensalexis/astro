## Why Proper Storage Matters

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
                Cannabis flower is delicate. The compounds that make it special — cannabinoids (like THC and CBD) and terpenes (the aromatic oils), [degrade](https://news.wsu.edu/press-release/2020/06/09/study-shows-cannabis-temporarily-relieves-ptsd-symptoms) when exposed to too much light, air, heat, or moisture.            
            

### Potency Drops

        
                THC slowly breaks down into CBN, which is sedating but lacks that uplifting kick you're looking for.             [Project CBD (cannabinoids)](https://projectcbd.org/).
            

### Flavor Fades

        
                

Terpenes evaporate, leaving your flower smelling like hay instead of citrus, pine, or spice. [Cleveland Clinic (marijuana)](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/drugs/21562-marijuana-cannabis).

            
            

### Mold Risk

        
                

Too much humidity equals moldy buds that are unsafe to smoke and must be discarded. [Cleveland Clinic (marijuana)](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/drugs/21562-marijuana-cannabis).

            
[Research](https://news.wsu.edu/press-release/2020/06/09/study-shows-cannabis-temporarily-relieves-ptsd-symptoms) shows that properly stored cannabis can remain stable for up to two years under the right conditions. The way you store your flower directly affects how enjoyable they are weeks or months later.

## Sweet Spot Conditions: Temp, Humidity, Light, Air

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
		

The “big four” enemies of fresh flower are heat, humidity, light, and oxygen. Here’s how to control each [CDC (cannabis and health)](https://www.cdc.gov/cannabis/index.html).

		
            

---

## Temperature

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
                

 	
- Best temperature for storing cannabis: **60–70°F (15–21°C)**.
 	
- Too hot? [Terpenes](https://news.wsu.edu/press-release/2020/06/09/study-shows-cannabis-temporarily-relieves-ptsd-symptoms) boil off faster. Too cold? Moisture condenses and ruins texture.
 	
- Rule of thumb: If you wouldn’t store wine there, don’t store weed there.

            
            

---

## Humidity

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
                

- Target **55–63% relative humidity (RH)**.**Below 50% = brittle buds that burn harshly.Above 65% = mold risk.**
- **Pro move**: Toss in a humidity pack (like Boveda or Integra) to keep RH dialed in automatically.

            
            

---

## Light

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
                

 	
- UV light is cannabis kryptonite. Studies show [UV breaks down THC molecules](https://news.wsu.edu/press-release/2020/06/09/study-shows-cannabis-temporarily-relieves-ptsd-symptoms), killing potency.
 	
- Always store in the dark: opaque jars, drawers, cupboards.

            
            

---

## Where in Your Home Should You Store It?

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
                

So you've got the right jar — now where do you put it? [NIH (PubMed)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/).

            
            

### Best Spots

        
                

Pantry, closet, drawer, or basement — anywhere cool, dark, and stable. [NIH (PubMed)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/).

            
            

### Avoid

        
                

Windowsills, car glove compartments, or anywhere near heat (ovens, radiators, electronics). [NIH (PubMed)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/).
