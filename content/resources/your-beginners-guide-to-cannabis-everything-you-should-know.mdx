## Understanding Key Compounds: THC, CBD & More

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
At the heart of cannabis are **cannabinoids**—chemical compounds that your body recognizes through the  [endocannabinoid system](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8221009/) (ECS). Two of the most famous are THC (tetrahydrocannabinol) and CBD (cannabidiol). 
THC is responsible for the “high,” while CBD is non-intoxicating and often linked with wellness. [Mayo Clinic (edibles overview)](https://www.mayoclinic.org/).
Beyond those, there are dozens of “[minor cannabinoids](https://cbd.org/learn/minor-cannabinoids-what-are-they)” like CBG (cannabigerol), CBC (cannabichromene), and CBN (cannabinol), each with its own budding research.

## How to Consume Cannabis: Methods, Onset & Duration

<ImagePlaceholder />

        

There are many ways to [use cannabis](https://kinebudsdispensary.com/blog/cannabis-education/flower-vs-edibles-vs-vapes-whats-right-for-you/), and each path leads to different onset times, effects, and durations. Here’s the breakdown:

																														
            

### Flower (Smoking)

        
[Flower](https://kinebudsdispensary.com/menu/categories/flower) is the classic way to enjoy cannabis. You roll it, pack it, light it, and inhale. Effects usually kick in within minutes and last a couple of hours. Smoking offers that traditional, full-bodied experience—aroma, flavor, and all—but keep in mind it also exposes your lungs to combustion byproducts like tar and ash.
																														
            

### Vapes

        
                [Vaping](https://kinebudsdispensary.com/menu/categories/vaporizers) skips the smoke and goes straight to vaporizing the oil or flower at controlled temperatures. The result? Fast-acting effects like smoking, but generally smoother on your lungs. It’s discreet, portable, and often comes in precise doses—perfect for those who prefer consistency without the lingering smell of smoke.
“Is vaping safer than smoking?” Vaping avoids combustion but still introduces inhalants—choose reputable products and trusted hardware.             [Cleveland Clinic (marijuana)](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/drugs/21562-marijuana-cannabis).
																														
            

### Edibles

        
                

Think [gummies, chocolates, and beverages](https://kinebudsdispensary.com/menu/categories/edibles). These travel through your digestive system, so expect a slower start (30 minutes to 2 hours or more), but the ride lasts much longer—sometimes 4–8 hours or more.

            
																														
            

### Tinctures, topicals, concentrates

        
                

[Tinctures](https://kinebudsdispensary.com/menu/categories/tinctures) go under the tongue and act faster than edibles but slower than inhalation. Topicals (creams/lotions) typically don’t create a “high” and focus on localized relief. [Concentrates](https://kinebudsdispensary.com/menu/categories/concentrates) deliver high potency fast, but they’re best for experienced users.

## Storing Your Cannabis: Keeping It Fresh

<ImagePlaceholder />

        

You’ve picked your favorites—now it’s time to [protect your investment](https://kinebudsdispensary.com/blog/cannabis-education/how-to-store-cannabis-flower-fresh/). Cannabis doesn’t like heat, light, or air, and improper storage can quickly turn premium flower or oil into a stale experience.

            

### For Flower

        
                

Store in airtight, opaque containers in a cool, dark place—a cabinet or drawer works perfectly. Avoid plastic bags (they crush trichomes) and sunlight (which breaks down THC and terpenes). Think of it like coffee beans: air, heat, and light are the enemies of freshness. [Mayo Clinic (edibles overview)](https://www.mayoclinic.org/).

            
            

### For Vape Carts

        
                

Keep cartridges upright with the cap on and [away from direct heat](https://kinebudsdispensary.com/blog/cannabis-education/how-long-do-vape-carts-last-before-they-go-bad/). Your car’s glove box or a sunny windowsill? Big no-nos. Excessive heat can cause leaks or degrade potency over time.

            
            

### For Edibles

        
                

Check the package for a “best by” date and store them in their original containers. Most last a few months unopened, but refrigeration can extend shelf life—just keep them away from curious kids or roommates. [Mayo Clinic (edibles overview)](https://www.mayoclinic.org/).
