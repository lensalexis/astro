## Flower vs Edibles vs Vapes: What's Right for You?

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
		Choosing cannabis isn't just about strain or potency ‚Äî it's about how you consume it. Whether you light up flower, pop an edible, or hit a vape, the experience can feel totally different. Some people want fast relief, others want long-lasting calm, and some just want convenience without the smell.So, which one's right for you? Let's break it down. ## How They Work: The Basics

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
																														
            

### Flower (smoking or dry herb vaping)

        
                When you smoke cannabis flower, cannabinoids like THC and CBD enter your bloodstream almost instantly through your lungs. That's why the effects hit within minutes. Expect 2‚Äì4 hours of results, depending on strain and tolerance.             [Mayo Clinic (edibles overview)](https://www.mayoclinic.org/).
																														
            

### Vapes

        
                

Instead of burning, vaporizers heat cannabis oil or flower until cannabinoids turn into vapor. This gives you a smoother inhale, faster onset (like smoking), and fewer toxins from combustion. Plus, vapes are discreet ‚Äî no lingering skunky cloud. [CDC (cannabis and health)](https://www.cdc.gov/cannabis/index.html).

            
																														
            

### Edibles

        
                

Edibles take the scenic route. THC travels through your digestive system and is processed by your liver into 11-hydroxy-THC, a stronger compound. That's why edibles hit harder and last longer. But it also means a delayed onset: anywhere from 30 minutes to 2 hours. [PubMed (THC pharmacokinetics)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=THC+pharmacokinetics+oral).

## Potency & Efficiency: Dose Smarter

<ImagePlaceholder />

        

When it comes to [cannabis](https://news.wsu.edu/press-release/2020/06/09/study-shows-cannabis-temporarily-relieves-ptsd-symptoms), how you consume it makes a huge difference in how strong it feels. Potency, efficiency, and how your body processes THC all play a role in shaping the experience. Here's a quick breakdown to help you dose smarter.

            

### Flower

        
                

Tops out around [25‚Äì30% THC](https://news.wsu.edu/press-release/2020/06/09/study-shows-cannabis-temporarily-relieves-ptsd-symptomshttps://cannabistraininguniversity.com/cannabis-101/thc-percentage/). The effects are noticeable but easier to manage since you can take one puff at a time.

            
            

### Vapes

        
                

Pack a punch. Many carts contain [60‚Äì90%](https://cannabistraininguniversity.com/cannabis-101/thc-percentage/) THC, so just one or two pulls might feel like smoking an entire joint. Efficiency is high, but so is the risk of overdoing it if you're new.

            
            

### Edibles

        
                

Feel the strongest to many users because of how the liver processes THC. That's why 10mg in a gummy can sometimes feel stronger than smoking half a gram. For beginners: start low (2.5‚Äì5mg) and wait before taking more. [NORML (policy overview)](https://norml.org/).
