## Who Can Legally Use Cannabis in New Jersey

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
            

### Adults 21+

        
                

Under New Jersey's Cannabis Regulatory, Enforcement Assistance, and Marketplace Modernization (CREAMM) Act, [adults 21 years or older](https://www.nj.gov/cannabis/adult-personal/) are legally allowed to purchase and use recreational cannabis.

            
            

### Out-of-State Visitors

        
                

Even out-of-state visitors aged 21 and above can purchase from licensed dispensaries, provided they present a valid, government-issued ID. [Mayo Clinic (edibles overview)](https://www.mayoclinic.org/).

            
            

### Medical Patients

        
                

There's also a medical cannabis program for patients with qualifying conditions. These patients, or their designated caregivers, can access specific products through licensed alternative treatment centers. [National Institute on Drug Abuse (cannabis)](https://nida.nih.gov/research-topics/marijuana).

            
				

When it comes to using, the law assumes you got your cannabis from a licensed retailer or delivery service—possession is only legal under those conditions. [Mayo Clinic (edibles overview)](https://www.mayoclinic.org/).

## How Much Cannabis You Can Buy & Possess in New Jersey

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
		

You can buy and possess cannabis, but only up to the legal limit. [PubMed Central (free full text)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/).

		
            

### How Much You Can Buy Per Transaction

        
                

New Jersey allows adults to buy up to 1 ounce of cannabis in any of these forms: [CDC (impaired driving)](https://www.cdc.gov/transportationsafety/impaired_driving/index.html).

            
            

### Flower

        
                

Up to 1 oz of flower (smokable cannabis) [Project CBD (cannabinoids)](https://projectcbd.org/).

            
            

### Concentrates

        
                

4 grams of THC concentrates (vape carts, resin, rosin, shatter) [PubMed Central (free full text)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/).

            
            

### Ingestibles

        
                

1,000 mg THC in ingestible products (gummies, chocolates, capsules, tinctures) [CDC (impaired driving)](https://www.cdc.gov/transportationsafety/impaired_driving/index.html).

            
            

### These limits apply to combined purchases, not each category individually.

        
                

You'll see dispensaries list "equivalency units" to help you stay compliant. [Project CBD (cannabinoids)](https://projectcbd.org/).

            
            

### Legal Possession Limits (Adults 21+)

        
                

When it comes to how much you can carry, the limit is much more generous: up to 6 ounces of cannabis or cannabis products is allowed on your person if you're 21 or older. [PubMed Central (free full text)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/).

            
		Crossing that 6-ounce threshold is not just illegal—it becomes a fourth-degree crime, with  [penalties](https://www.findlaw.com/state/new-jersey-law/new-jersey-marijuana-laws.html/) that could include up to 18 months in prison and a fine as high as $25,000.

## What You Cannot Do With Cannabis in New Jersey

<ImagePlaceholder />

        
		

Here’s what you can’t do with legal weed in NJ. [Cleveland Clinic (marijuana)](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/drugs/21562-marijuana-cannabis).

		
									

### You CANNOT smoke or vape in public

		This includes: [CDC (impaired driving)](https://www.cdc.gov/transportationsafety/impaired_driving/index.html).

- Sidewalks

- Beaches

- Parks

- Boardwalks

- Public housing

- Public transportation

- Bars, restaurants, or venues (unless they're licensed consumption lounges)

Violations generally result in a civil fine, though repeated violations can escalate.		 [NHTSA (drug-impaired driving)](https://www.nhtsa.gov/risky-driving/drug-impaired-driving).
									

### You CANNOT drive while high

		Driving under the influence of cannabis is illegal and treated similarly to alcohol DUI. Police can conduct roadside impairment evaluations. THC levels aren't measured via breathalyzer; impairment is judged through observation and testing.If convicted, penalties may include: [ScienceDaily (cannabis news)](https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/mind_brain/cannabis/).

- License suspension

- Fines

- Criminal charges in severe cases

		
									

### You CANNOT drive with open cannabis packaging

		Open-container rules apply to cannabis in NJ. Keep cannabis: [NIH (PubMed)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/).

- In the original, sealed package

- In the trunk or glove compartment

		
									

### You CANNOT cross state lines

		Even if the bordering state also legalized cannabis, interstate transport is illegal under federal law. This includes: [CDC (impaired driving)](https://www.cdc.gov/transportationsafety/impaired_driving/index.html).

- Driving cannabis to Pennsylvania, New York, Connecticut, Delaware (DE)

- Flying with cannabis out of Newark or Atlantic City

- Shipping cannabis by mail

		
									

### You CANNOT sell cannabis without a license

		

Any sale outside of licensed dispensaries is a crime — even "gifting with required donation" models. [CDC (impaired driving)](https://www.cdc.gov/transportationsafety/impaired_driving/index.html).

		
									

### You CANNOT consume where smoking tobacco is banned

		This applies to: [Mayo Clinic (edibles overview)](https://www.mayoclinic.org/).

- Hotels

- Workplaces

- Public buildings

- Transportation hubs

Public smoking bans extend to cannabis unless a venue explicitly allows it.		 [National Institute on Drug Abuse (cannabis)](https://nida.nih.gov/research-topics/marijuana).
